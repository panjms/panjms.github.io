Bibimbap

A quick and refreshing medley balancing sweet, savory, and spicy. Great for using fridge leftovers!

Serves 3-4

Prep time 20 min

Cook time 25 min

Total time 55 min

Bibimbap is a very popular Korean dish that you are likely to find at most Korean restaurants! It is extremely adaptable with its various toppings, meaning you can mix and match for you and your crowd. Originally a dish created to minimize waste of leftover vegetables and side dishes, this meal remains a cost effective way to create a vibrant and delicious dish!

The ingredients are fairly simple and do not require super fancy preparation, but neatness goes a long way in its presentation! Carefully and individually preparing each ingredient will make the colors and textures stand out when assembled in the final bowl.

To make the dish extra tasty or even more special, consider using a dolsot, a personal sized bowl out of agalmatolite. When heated up to serve bibimbap, the dolsot will create a delicious and crispy layer of scorched rice at the bottom. Hearing all the ingredients sizzle away at the table is another plus!

Bibimbap Recipe

    Special Equipment

  • mandoline
  • dolsot (individual sized stoneware for serving)

    • Ingredients

  • 3 cups white rice

    • For the Beef

  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 lb beef (ribeye, sirloin, or brisket)

    • Toppings

  • 1 large carrot
  • 2 medium zucchini
  • 1 large cucumber
  • 1 bunch spinach
  • 3 cups dried shiitake mushroom (soaked in warm water for at least 30 min to rehydrate)
  • 3 cups bean sprout
  • 1 package pressed tofu
  • 1-2 eggs per person
  • gochujang (to serve with)
  • diced scallions

Directions

  1. For the beef: Mince the beef or cut into thin slices (depending on preference). Add minced garlic, salt, black pepper, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Stir to evenly coat. Cover and set aside.
  2. For the rice: Wash rice and drain. Prepare following instructions as per the rice cooker (or follow guidelines for stovetop methods). Heat dolsot in an oven at 350°F.
  3. For the toppings: Wash all vegetables and set aside. Peel carrots (and others if desired, but not necessary). Using a mandoline, julienne separately the carrots, zucchini, and cucumbers. Remove mushrooms from water and squeeze to extract excess moisture. Slice thinly. Remove tofu from package, rinse, and dry before slicing into thin strips. Heat oil in a thick bottomed pan over medium-high heat. Saut√© the vegetables in batches in the following order: mushroom, zucchini, cucumber, bean sprouts, tofu, carrots, and lastly spinach. In a non-stick pan, heat oil on high to fry eggs sunny-side-up. If desired, raw egg yolk can be separated from the whites and added directly as a topping instead.
  4. Using the same pan as for the vegetables, heat oil on high. Brown beef and remove when most of the liquid in the pan is gone, about 3 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.
  5. Remove dolsot from oven. Add rice and top with vegetables and beef. Place fried egg (or raw egg yolk) on top and garnish with gochujang and scallions if desired. Serve immediately.
