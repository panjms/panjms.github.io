Homemade Espresso Recipe

  • Cafelat Robot manual espresso lever
  • coffee grinder
  • kitchen scale

    • Ingredients

  • 17g coffee beans
  • 60g water

Directions

  1. For the beef: Mince the beef or cut into thin slices (depending on preference). Add minced garlic, salt, black pepper, soy sauce, and sesame oil. Stir to evenly coat. Cover and set aside.
  2. For the rice: Wash rice and drain. Prepare following instructions as per the rice cooker (or follow guidelines for stovetop methods). Heat dolsot in an oven at 350°F.
  3. For the toppings: Wash all vegetables and set aside. Peel carrots (and others if desired, but not necessary). Using a mandoline, julienne separately the carrots, zucchini, and cucumbers. Remove mushrooms from water and squeeze to extract excess moisture. Slice thinly. Remove tofu from package, rinse, and dry before slicing into thin strips. Heat oil in a thick bottomed pan over medium-high heat. Saut√© the vegetables in batches in the following order: mushroom, zucchini, cucumber, bean sprouts, tofu, carrots, and lastly spinach. In a non-stick pan, heat oil on high to fry eggs sunny-side-up. If desired, raw egg yolk can be separated from the whites and added directly as a topping instead.
  4. Using the same pan as for the vegetables, heat oil on high. Brown beef and remove when most of the liquid in the pan is gone, about 3 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.
  5. Remove dolsot from oven. Add rice and top with vegetables and beef. Place fried egg (or raw egg yolk) on top and garnish with gochujang and scallions if desired. Serve immediately.
